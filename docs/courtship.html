Courtship

So used to your eyes averting from me,

the affixed gaze you save for the distance

where you keep your grievances

feels a costume now attuned to me.

Are you possessed?

Shapeshifting sheik from my visions

I watch for the shotgun scatter aura

a reddish plasma

but I find only you.

“I’m into you” more your breath than voice

thermochromic iris bridging light and dark.

I manage to order a beer I’m slow

to raise, slow with my lip on the rim.

You capture frame by frame

fresh language my movements speak

everything reversed in your plastic specs

my brown skin muddled

words not sounds but flashes

white of teeth spelling in morse.

We spend dinner suffocating.

Speckles of saliva fall slower

linger with our sweat mid-air

dense sweat spit bubble we’re in

and if we stay any longer

we’ll suffocate the whole restaurant.
