How to Make Samosas

I. Ingredients

1 full-time job at who-even-cares

1 one-month vacation at your parents’ house

1 aged Indian mother (48-60 yrs)

2 ½ knacks for fucking up

1 pair of Plaid pajamas

1 pair of Bugs Bunny slippers (optional)

1 group of old friends to avoid like the avian flu

II. Preparation

First, prepare the mother. The aging process

is crucial; after picking, it takes 24-25 years for mothers to age

just enough. When you’ve reached thirteen years, pick up

smoking pot. Stay generally stoned for about five

years, but make sure to quit well before starting

the samosas; otherwise, the mother rots.

Before transitioning from the pot phase, get into

a four-year college. This will begin the healing

process which extracts the flavor. Trust settles

along the edges and the worry lines fade. She’ll say

“Why not be a lawyer?” and “Can you do me a small

favor?” This is when you turn the temperature down,

and let her simmer on low heat for a while. At your graduation

ceremony, when she weeps with no restraint into your dad’s shoulder,

immediately remove from stove and let cool.

Get a full-time job and move out almost immediately.

Maintain a mostly absent presence, calling home maybe

once a month. Your family should suffer a major loss

within the next couple of years. Tell your boss

you need to be home. Mix all of the ingredients

together in a living room sitting on the couch

with the mother. Make sure she’s watching T.V.,

something she enjoys watching, but can ultimately

do without, like Three’s Company re-runs.

Schmooze the mother for approximately 5-10 min.

The mother should sigh, “Fine,” when ready.

III. Procedure

Leave it to the mother.

You’ll never make samosas

like she does.
