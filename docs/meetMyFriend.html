Tacos were bland. Chips were stale. White picket fence, white string lights, white folks. We sat waiting for the check.

The sun dove behind buildings and I could watch the shadows morph. My attention was just an ornament to the conversation they were having. I made a few remarks like nervous ticks. Nothing they nodded to.

Her invites for my participation left me feeling even more estranged. I crawled into my stomach when she laughed at his jokes harder than at mine. Pushed my sunglasses further up my nose so she wouldn’t notice my pain. Mirrored his movements to borrow his appeal. Reformed my throat and squeezed my nasal passage to get his voice just right.

As the shadows stretched, my skin blended into them. She glanced to me at her right and didn’t seem to mind me disappearing.

My arm on the table faded enough that I could see the napkin and table through it. As the color of my clothes and skin started floating into cloud formations, she waved her hand through me, got me started in the direction of the sky. The remaining food on my plate seemed more appealing to her now than when I offered. She moved the plate in front of her and dug at everything that remained.
